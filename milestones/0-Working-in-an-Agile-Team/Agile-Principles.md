However, a major challenge can be the lack of a fixed long-term roadmap, which requires high levels of discipline and constant communication among team members to prevent scope creep.

**Scrum vs. Kanban**

**Scrum** is like a "timed race." The team commits to a specific amount of work (a Sprint) and has a set deadline (usually 2 weeks) to finish it. It uses structured ceremonies like Sprint Planning and Retrospectives to keep everyone on track.

**Kanban** is like a "flowing river." There are no fixed start or end dates for a "sprint." Instead, the focus is on visualizing the work on a board and moving tasks from "To-Do" to "Done" as fast as possible, constantly managing the flow to prevent bottlenecks.

The **Agile principle** that will be most useful in my work as a Mobile Developer is:

*Deliver working software frequently, from a couple of weeks to a couple of months, with a preference to the shorter timescale.*

This principle is essential because it encourages me to deliver small but incremental steps of improvement. By implementing a working batch of code frequently, I can get faster debugging and feedback on my development tasks. This iterative approach reduces the risk of creating major bugs in my final submissions and ensures that my work on the Focus Bear repository remains aligned with user needs.
