# Reflection

To be more inclusive, I will prioritize direct and clear communication by avoiding the use of jargon words and figurative language that could be misinterpreted. I will provide information in a detailed, structure formats for teammates who may struggle with large blocks of text. I will also respect each other's way of communication and working styles without interrupting them.

One major pitfall is an overwhelming UI with bright colors which can lead to overload for users. Another is an unclear navigation such as inconsistent button placements which creates frustration to users. By avoiding these design and tone errors, we can ensure the tool remains a supportive partner rather than a source of stress.

In my coding and documentation tasks, I can implement "Alt-text" for all diagrams and ensure that any user-facing messages are written at a simple reading level. By testing my software with accessibility tools from the start, I can catch potential barriers to entry before they affect neurodivergent users.

## First-Person Account

*Source*: [How to ADHD: Why is it so Hard to Do Something That Seems Easy?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Uo08uS904Rg)

*What I Learned*: I learned about the "Wall of Awful," which is the emotional barrier (shame, failure, and anxiety) that builds up around simple tasks for neurodivergent individuals. It taught me that a productivity tool shouldn't just provide a "To-Do" list; it needs to help the user manage the emotions associated with starting a task. This is why Focus Bear’s guided routines are so effective—they lower the emotional height of that wall.

**Hypothetical Supportive Response**
*A user sends a message saying they feel like a failure because they couldn't finish their morning routine for three days in a row and feel overwhelmed by the app's reminders.*

**My Response:** *"Hi there, I want to start by saying it is completely okay that you missed your routine. Life happens, and your value isn't tied to a streak in an app. Sometimes our brains just need a different pace, and Focus Bear is here to support you, not judge you. When you're ready, maybe we can try starting with just one small 5-minute task today? There's no rush to get back to the full routine—we can take it one step at a time whenever you feel up to it."*
