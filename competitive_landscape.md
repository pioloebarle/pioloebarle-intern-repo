**Reflection**

Focus Bear distinguishes itself by having features not only focused on passive restriction such as blocking and restricting specific application to focus on your work but also guiding users to build healthy habits. Focus Bear takes a holistic approach.

I would choose Focus Bear since it integrates productivity and wellbeing of the users. Its commitment to accessibility for those with ADHD and Autism makes it feel like a supportive partner rather than just a restrictive utility tool.

Apps like **Tiimo** and **Routinery** offer more visual, timeline-based scheduling that allows users to see their entire day at a glance through colorful, icon-heavy interfaces. This visual time-mapping can sometimes be more intuitive for people who need to see the "shape" of their day to feel organized.

Focus Bear could benefit from more customizable visual themes or icon sets for routines, similar to Tiimo, to make the interface more engaging and less utilitarian. As a mobile developer, I believe adding these "gamified" visual elements could improve user retention and make the daily routine feel more rewarding.
