**Reflection**

The primary benefit of Agile is the ability to adapt quickly to changing requirements through continuous feedback, ensuring the team always works on the highest-value tasks. However, a major challenge can be the lack of a fixed long-term roadmap, which requires high levels of discipline and constant communication among team members to prevent scope creep.

Agile principles encourage me to deliver small but incremental steps of improvement by implementing working batch of code frequently, which allows for faster debugging and feedback on my development tasks. This iterative approaches reduces the risk of creating major bugs in my final submissions and ensures that my work on the **Focus Bear** repository reamins aligned with user needs.
